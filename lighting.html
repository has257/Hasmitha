Lighting Wiring

A lighting circuit is a dedicated electrical circuit that supplies power to lighting fixtures within a specific area. It includes essential components like conductors, switches, protection devices, and the lighting fixtures themselves.There are four types of lighting wiring:

  • Switch-controlled lighting
  • Dimmer switch lighting
  • Motion sensor lighting
  • Outdoor lighting

Lighting circuits are wired in two different ways:

  • Junction Boxes: The modern lighting junction box is where electric wires originate. It connects the wires to the cord set and supports the light fixture by attaching the ceiling canopy.
  • Loop-in ceiling roses: The main circuit cable from the consumer unit loops in and out of multi-terminal ceiling roses from which individual cables run to the light switches.
